How to Quit Drinking

  • Set a clear goal — choose a quit date and write it down
  • Remove triggers — avoid places, people, or situations that encourage drinking
  • Tell someone you trust — support increases success
  • Replace the habit — exercise, hobbies, journaling, or social activities
  • Track progress — apps, notes, or daily check‑ins
  • Seek professional help — counselors, therapists, or support groups
  • Know setbacks happen — a slip is not a failure

Hotlines & Resources

  • SAMHSA National Helpline — 1‑800‑662‑HELP, Free confidential help for substance use and mental health.
  • Crisis Text Line — Text HOME to 741741
  • National Suicide & Crisis Lifeline — Call or text 988
  • Local school counselor — private, supportive, and trained
  • Alcoholics Anonymous — free meetings everywhere
  • Al‑Anon / Alateen — support for teens affected by someone else’s drinking

Cost of Addiction

  • Financial cost — buying alcohol, medical bills, legal fees
  • Health cost — liver disease, heart problems, long‑term brain changes
  • Emotional cost — stress, guilt, anxiety, depression
  • Relationship cost — conflict, broken trust, isolation
  • Opportunity cost — missed school, jobs, or activities
  • Legal cost — DUIs, fines, court fees

“Ticket to Death”

Drunk Driving

Slowed reaction time → can’t brake or steer fast enough

Poor judgment → risky decisions

Fatal crashes — alcohol is involved in ~30% of U.S. traffic deaths

Liver Disease

Fatty liver → early damage

Hepatitis → inflammation

Cirrhosis → permanent scarring, can be fatal

Alcohol Poisoning

Breathing slows

Heart rate drops

Loss of consciousness

Can be deadly
